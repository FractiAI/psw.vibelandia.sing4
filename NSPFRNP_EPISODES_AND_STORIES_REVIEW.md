## 3. EPISODE STRUCTURE (FORM OF EVERY EPISODE)

**Per Chairman directive and REALITY_SERIES_FORMAT:**

- **Variable length by mission:** Episodes (and series runs) are **variable length depending on missions**. 60 minutes / 12,000 words = full rich target when the mission calls for it; missions can dictate shorter or longer. All three parallel series follow this rule.
- **Can append:** Episodes and series runs **can append** — content can be added to existing episodes or runs over time; extend in place, no need to replace.
- **Duration (full rich target):** 60 minutes per episode when mission warrants full episode.
- **Word count (full rich target):** 12,000 words per episode when mission warrants; otherwise as mission dictates.
- **Format:** Fully scripted — not outlines. Full dialogue and beats (scale by mission).
- **Sections:**

| Section    | Screen time | Word target   |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Cold Open | 2–3 min     | ~800–1,200    |
| Act 1     | 10–15 min   | ~3,000–3,600  |
| Act 2     | 10–15 min   | ~3,000–3,600  |
| Act 3     | 10–15 min   | ~3,000–3,600  |
| Tag       | 2–3 min     | ~800–1,200    |
| **Total** | **60 min**  | **12,000 words** |

**Beats by section:**

1. **Cold Open:** Hook, preview, countdown.
2. **Act 1:** Main story, character development.
3. **Act 2:** Conflict, challenges, development.
4. **Act 3:** Resolution, transformation, happy ending.
5. **Tag:** Countdown update, next episode tease. *That is tomorrow's episode — always tease new, laying the edge for today's seed.*

**Ref:** [EPISODES_RICH_60_MINUTE_FULLY_SCRIPTED_DIRECTIVE.md](EPISODES_RICH_60_MINUTE_FULLY_SCRIPTED_DIRECTIVE.md) · [REALITY_SERIES_FORMAT.md](REALITY_SERIES_FORMAT.md)

## 3b. STORY-TELLING CONTENT MIX (NSPFRNP) — 25% × 4

**Every episode weaves in four 25% content streams.** Include these in story-telling NSPFRNP so they are visible and used:

| 25% | Content stream | What it is |
|-----|----------------|------------|
| **25% Normal stages** | Production / story stages, day-in-the-life, standard beats | On-stage action, club/campus beats, arrival, discovery, celebration, countdown — the “normal” stage action viewers see. |
| **25% Cursor AI / NSPFRNP interactions** | User–agent dialogue, MCA in action, session capture | Scenes or intercuts: user at laptop in Cursor; agent (NSPFRNP) running; dialogue or on-screen session text; metabolize → crystallize → animate in the room. Conversations as content. |
| **25% WhatsApp / social / texts** | Girlfriends, social media, vendors, team on rollout | WhatsApp chats with girlfriends; social media interactions (posts, DMs, stories); texts with vendors and with team on rollout. Digital-platform storytelling — real life flowing into the episode. |
| **25% Traditional (club / campus / WINK / vibes)** | Existing content types | Strip club bathroom / Men’s Club, Vibes & Black Holes, Countdown Gaming, Day in the Life on Campus, WINK Episodes — overlapping as in REALITY_SERIES_FORMAT. |

**Practice:** Weave all four into every episode where applicable. Normal stages = the main stage. Cursor AI = the making-of / NSPFRNP-in-action layer. WhatsApp/social/texts = the real-life pipeline (girlfriends, vendors, team rollout). Traditional = club, campus, countdown, WINK, vibes.

## 6. EPISODES BY FLOW, NOT DAYS

**Flow** = narrative flow, content flow, MCA flow. Episodes are ordered and spun by **flow** — what story beats and content flow next — not strictly by calendar day.

**Days** = still used as timeline/countdown label (e.g. Day 50 of 52 → Finale March 20, 2026). Days inform countdown and SYNTH price; they do **not** lock episode order. Episode order and "next episode" are driven by **flow**.

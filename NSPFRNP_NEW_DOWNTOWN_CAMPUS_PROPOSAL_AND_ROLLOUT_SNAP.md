### B. Research the Target Downtown · Score for Vibeability · Full Connect (NSPFRNP for All)

**Every downtown campus proposal must research the target downtown, score it for vibeability, and integrate that research into the proposal so the proposal is fully connected to the downtown's reality and hot spots.** Include in NSPFRNP for all.

#### 1. Research the target downtown

- **Geography & boundaries:** Downtown footprint; streets; blocks; zoning; pedestrian corridors.
- **Decay indicators:** Vacancy rates; boarded windows; high-water marks; “get away” narrative; low tide evidence; empty shells; barnacles (literal or figurative).
- **Assets:** Existing venues (clubs, theaters, halls, restaurants, hotels); landmarks; parks; transit nodes; parking; utilities.
- **Demographics & economy:** Population; daytime vs. nighttime; employment; retail; nightlife; tourism; collapse/revival signals.
- **History & narrative:** Why the tide went out; local stories; first people nations and place names; cultural anchors; music, arts, food heritage.
- **Hot spots (real):** Name real streets, real venues, real nodes — e.g. Virginia Street, Reno Men's Club, Silver Legacy, El Dorado, Lucky 7; or Ponce plaza, El Gran Sol, specific bars/theaters. No generic placeholder; the proposal must name the downtown's actual reality and hot spots.

#### 2. Score for vibeability

Score the target downtown on the following (use a 0–10 or Red / Yellow / Green per dimension). Reference: [ONE_PAGER_WHAT_MAKES_A_VIBABLE_CAMPUS.md](ONE_PAGER_WHAT_MAKES_A_VIBABLE_CAMPUS.md).

| Dimension | What to score | Use in proposal |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Contribution density potential** | Can we concentrate creators, performers, VIPs, vibers? Existing clusters? | High score → vibeable campus design fits; low → build narrative for “first cluster.” |
| **Walkability** | Pedestrian connectivity; safe walking; no cars needed in core. | Minimum for vibeable campus. |
| **Decay vs. potential** | Low tide visible but reversible; empty shells + some still alive; “get away” but not total collapse. | Core scene for the story. |
| **Safe free vibing readiness** | Layout/rules can assume vibing state; no mixing with heavy traffic/machinery in core. | Design brief for campus. |
| **Hot spots count & quality** | Number and strength of real venues, streets, nodes we can name. | Proposal names them; full connect to reality. |
| **First people / local narrative** | Strength of first people nations, local culture, place story. | Collaborative western; honor and partner. |
| **Anchor event feasibility** | Can we name a six-day arc and anchor event (e.g. Super Bowl Sunday, festival, launch)? | Timeline and edge. |

**Output:** A short **Vibeability Score** (e.g. overall 0–10 or R/Y/G) and a 1–2 page **Downtown Research Summary** — geography, decay, assets, hot spots (named), vibeability scores, recommended anchor event. Integrate this summary into the proposal so the proposal is **full connect to the downtown's reality and hot spots**.

#### 3. Integrate into the proposal (full connect)

- **Proposal text:** Weave in real street names, real venues, real hot spots, real first people/local narrative from the research. The one-liner and all materials must feel specific to [downtown name], not generic.
- **Surfaces:** Downtown Research Summary + Vibeability Score live alongside the one-pager and roll-out; link from [interfaces/new-downtown-campus-proposal.html](interfaces/new-downtown-campus-proposal.html) and from Office Hours / Launch Pad when a target downtown is selected.

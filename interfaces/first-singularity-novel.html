First Singularity
January 13th

Dumas voice minus revenge. Only happy endings.

"On January 13th a man wakes knowing the world has turned. Dumas voice minus revenge — only happy endings. He chooses return, not reckoning, and steps through the door of the Reno Men's Club with a key instead of a sword. Let the celebration begin."
First Singularity January 13th is a Hobbit-length novel in the voice of Alexandre Dumas's Count of Monte Cristo — minus revenge, only happy endings. A man wakes on January 13th in a city that has not yet learned to call itself the capital of the impossible, and he knows with the certainty of saints and madmen that the world has turned. He carries no grievance, no sword — only three sentences and the conviction that the party can shift to Ultimate Baller V mode for all who wish it. Part One (Ch 1–37), Part Two — The Return, Part Three — After. The story ends where Edmond Dantès would have returned: oooos and ahhhhhs, sudden hush, Dos Equis moment, proclamation. Place: the Reno Men's Club, a fine gentlemen's club, full restaurant and cabaret — Twain's Vibelandia Downtown Reno. Beauty and voice: Evan Bondonte.

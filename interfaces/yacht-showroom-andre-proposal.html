Bridge integration

Bridge Integration provides a non-invasive overlay to the existing wheelhouse. No removal of installed ECDIS, radar, or autopilot. Interface is via NMEA 2000 and NMEA 0183; compatible with Furuno NavNet, Garmin Marine, Raymarine Axiom/Lighthouse, Simrad GO/NSS/EVO. Single or dual redundant CAN bus connection. Display output: HDMI 2.0 and/or DisplayPort; resolution support up to 4K. Touch, voice, and gesture control; voice commands usable in 40+ dB bridge ambient. Mounting: flush or pedestal; shock and vibration per IEC 60945; operating temperature -15°C to +55°C; IP54 front panel. Failover: console can run in read-only mode if primary nav sources drop; no takeover of helm or critical safety systems. Captain retains full override. Installation: typically one to three days depending on vessel; no class society re-approval required for add-on when existing equipment remains primary.

GPS & navigation

GPS & Navigation layer sits above existing GNSS (GPS/GLONASS/Galileo/BeiDou), AIS transponder/receiver, and radar. Consumes standard NMEA 0183/2000 sentences (GGA, RMC, VTG, AIVDM, etc.). Outputs: routes and waypoints to ECDIS/plotter in standard format; no lock-in. Enhanced features: multi-criteria route optimization (fuel, time, weather, traffic); weather overlay (GRIB, METAR, buoy data); post-singularity layer adds EGS pipe and Schumann-layer awareness and real-time telemetry for extended-range and new-destination pathfinding. No replacement of primary nav sensors; augmentation only. Fix quality and integrity monitoring; alerting on degradation. Suitable for ocean, coastal, and inland; waypoint capacity and route legs per vessel class. Data refresh: 1 Hz base; 10 Hz optional for high-speed craft.
